# Iron-Recycling Macrophages in Prenatal Human Skin

## Summary

Iron-recycling macrophages are one of four macrophage subsets identified in prenatal human skin (7–17 post-conception weeks) by Gopee et al. (2024), alongside LYVE1+, MHCII+, and TREM2+ microglia-like (TML) macrophages. They are defined by a conserved transcriptomic signature found across multiple developing organs and play roles in both iron homeostasis and skin vascular morphogenesis.

## Markers

Iron-recycling macrophages are annotated using marker genes identified consistently across developing organs by Suo et al. (2022). The supplementary data of Gopee et al. (2024) explicitly lists:

> **"Iron-recycling macrophages: CD5L, APOE, VCAM, TIMD4, SLC40A1"**
>
> — Gopee et al. (2024), Supplementary Materials, Extended Data Fig. 6e

These markers reflect the subset's functional specialisation:

- **SLC40A1** (ferroportin) — the sole known cellular iron exporter, central to iron recycling after erythrophagocytosis
- **TIMD4** — a phosphatidylserine receptor that mediates efferocytosis (clearance of apoptotic cells and senescent erythrocytes)
- **CD5L** — a scavenger receptor involved in macrophage lipid metabolism and innate immune regulation
- **APOE** — mediates lipid transport and is implicated in phagocytic clearance
- **VCAM** — vascular cell adhesion molecule, consistent with this subset's role in endothelial cell chemotaxis

The classification originates from Suo et al. (2022), who defined eight macrophage groupings across organs:

> "Among macrophages, we identified eight broad macrophage groupings based on their transcriptome profile: 'LYVE1 hi', 'iron-recycling', 'MHC class II hi', 'Kupffer-like', 'microglia-like TREM2 hi', 'osteoclasts', and 'proliferating' macrophages."
>
> — Suo et al. (2022)

## Location

### In prenatal skin

Visium spatial transcriptomics deconvolution placed iron-recycling macrophages primarily in **microenvironment ME2**, alongside LYVE1+ macrophages:

> "Iron-recycling and LYVE1+ macrophages [contribute highest] to ME2"
>
> — Gopee et al. (2024), Supplementary Materials

They also co-localise with endothelial cells in neurovascular microenvironments ME1 and ME5:

> "Visium deconvolution analysis predicted co-location of prenatal skin macrophages with endothelial cells ('early and late neurovascular microenvironments', ME1 and ME5)"
>
> — Gopee et al. (2024)

### Across organs and in adults

Suo et al. (2022) showed that the adult counterpart of prenatal iron-recycling macrophages is the **erythrophagocytic macrophage** population, found primarily in **spleen and liver**:

> "erythrophagocytic macrophages... primarily from the spleen and liver that carry out iron-recycling functions"
>
> — Suo et al. (2022)

### Gestational timing

Iron-recycling macrophages are enriched in **later stages of gestation**, where they upregulate immune effector genes:

> "a large fraction of neighborhoods within the iron-recycling and MHCII hi macrophage populations were enriched in later stages of gestation. We found that these subpopulations upregulated genes encoding for immune effector functions"
>
> — Suo et al. (2022)

## Function

### 1. Endothelial cell chemotaxis

The most distinctive role of iron-recycling macrophages in prenatal skin is promoting endothelial cell chemotaxis, distinguishing them from the other macrophage subsets:

> "Gene module expression profiles suggested that sprouting angiogenesis (growth of new vessels) was promoted by LYVE1+ and TML macrophages, blood vessel morphogenesis by LYVE1+ macrophages and **endothelial cell chemotaxis by iron-recycling macrophages**"
>
> — Gopee et al. (2024)

Predicted ligand–receptor interactions supported reciprocal communication between macrophages and endothelial cells:

> "Predicted ligand-receptor interactions were consistent with reciprocal communication between macrophages and endothelial cells to support angiogenesis, chemotaxis and cell migration (for example, CXCL8-ACKR1 and CCL8-ACKR1)"
>
> — Gopee et al. (2024)

### 2. Angiogenesis

All four macrophage subsets, including iron-recycling, expressed angiogenic gene programs:

> "The four macrophage subsets (LYVE1+, MHC-II+, TML and iron-recycling) expressed gene programmes driving angiogenesis by gene ontology analysis (Supplementary Tables 23-26)"
>
> — Gopee et al. (2024), Supplementary Materials

The importance of macrophage-endothelial crosstalk for angiogenesis was validated experimentally: skin organoids (SkOs) lacking immune cells had markedly reduced endothelial cell heterogeneity, and co-culture with iPSC-derived macrophages rescued vascular network organisation:

> "A more elaborate and organized vascular network was seen in SkOs co-cultured with macrophages compared with control SkOs without macrophages"
>
> — Gopee et al. (2024)

### 3. Iron recycling and erythrophagocytosis

The canonical function of this subset — shared with its adult splenic/hepatic counterpart — is the phagocytic clearance of senescent erythrocytes and recycling of iron. This is reflected in the marker profile: SLC40A1 exports recycled iron, TIMD4 mediates recognition of phosphatidylserine on apoptotic cells, and CD5L/APOE support scavenging and lipid processing (Suo et al., 2022).

### 4. Immune effector function in later gestation

As gestation progresses, iron-recycling macrophages transition toward immune effector roles, upregulating genes associated with immune function (Suo et al., 2022). This temporal shift suggests a dual developmental trajectory: tissue-remodelling roles in early gestation, with increasing immunological competence later.
