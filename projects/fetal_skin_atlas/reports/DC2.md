# DC2 (Dendritic Cell Type 2)
Atlas: Early life tolerance depends on a subset of specialized dendritic cells and is reinforced by the skin microbiota (DOI: [10.1101/2022.06.23.497363](https://doi.org/10.1101/2022.06.23.497363))
Scope: adult

## Summary

DC2, also referred to as Dendritic Cell Type 2, is a prominent subset of dendritic cells present in human skin, particularly abundant in the dermal compartment. Characterized by the expression of key markers such as CD1C, FCER1A, and CLEC10A, DC2 are specialized antigen-presenting cells involved in both immunogenic and tolerogenic responses. Functional studies in both human and murine skin have revealed these cells to be adept at phagocytosis, antigen-presenting, and promoting the generation of regulatory T cells (Tregs), thus playing a vital role in establishing early-life immune tolerance, especially toward commensal skin microbiota. Their unique gene expression and regulatory capabilities distinguish DC2 from other dendritic cell populations, with ongoing research highlighting intra-population heterogeneity and specialized roles in immune regulation.

## Markers

Several specific markers define the DC2 population, as supported by cluster annotation and differential expression analysis:

- **CD1C**: Recognized as a canonical marker for DC2, CD1C is used extensively for identifying these cells in both immunological studies and atlas-level single-cell data (Gopee et al., 2024).
- **FCER1A**: This gene, encoding the alpha chain of the high-affinity IgE receptor, is also used to annotate DC2 clusters and suggests a role for these cells in modulating allergic responses (Gopee et al., 2024).
- **CLEC10A**: Identified as a top differentially expressed gene (DEG) in DC2 clusters, CLEC10A encodes a C-type lectin involved in glycan recognition, contributing to immune surveillance and pathogen recognition (Gopee et al., 2024).
- **CD163**: Another DEG for DC2, CD163 is typically associated with anti-inflammatory functions and phagocytosis, supporting the tolerogenic potential of this subset (Gopee et al., 2024).

Additional markers identified in the literature include CD301b (MGL2), S100A8/A9, CD64, CD115, and, in some subsets, low/intermediate levels of BDCA3 (L. Heger et al., 2020; E. Aa et al., 2018). The expression of monocyte-associated markers such as CD14 and CD163 in certain DC2 subsets suggests intra-population heterogeneity.

## Location

### Skin (Dermal Compartment)

DC2 are predominantly located in the dermis of human skin and are rarely detected in the epidermis (Gopee et al., 2024). Their spatial localization places them in a key position to interact with environmental antigens and commensal microbiota.

> "DC2 were localized mainly in the dermis, rarely found in the epidermis"
>
> — Gopee et al. (2024)

### Developmental Timing

Evidence highlights a functionally distinct subset of DC2 in neonatal skin, enriched for phagocytic and tolerogenic programs during early life, which may differ from later adult stages:

> "a functionally distinct subset of CD301b + type 2 conventional DC (DC2) in neonatal skin. Enriched for phagocytic, maturation as well as tolerance markers, these cells were revealed to be uniquely equipped to uptake skin bacteria, present their antigens and generate commensalspecific Tregs."
>
> — Antonin Weckel et al. (2022)

## Function

### Antigen Uptake and Presentation

DC2 are potent antigen-presenting cells capable of sampling environmental and commensal microbial antigens.

> "We show in skin that expression of CD301b in DC2 strongly correlates with that of other phagocytic receptors and that these cells are efficient at bacterial uptake."
>
> — Antonin Weckel et al. (2022)

DC2, especially those expressing the marker CD301b, demonstrate preferential uptake of commensal bacteria and efficient transport of antigens from the skin to draining lymph nodes:

> "This revealed that human DC2 accounted for the majority of commensal-laden skin DCs ( Fig. 3F-G)."
>
> — Antonin Weckel et al. (2022)

### Immune Regulation and Tolerance

A central function of skin DC2, particularly during neonatal development, is the generation of regulatory T cells and the maintenance of peripheral tolerance. This regulatory function is tightly linked to their ability to produce retinoic acid via the RALDH2 enzyme and the expression of other tolerogenic markers.

> "CD301b + DC2 as a critical cell population that supports early life establishment of peripheral tolerance to skin commensal bacteria."
>
> — Antonin Weckel et al. (2022)

> "CD301b + DC2 demonstrate a unique propensity for Treg generation."
>
> — Antonin Weckel et al. (2022)

> "The regulatory capacity of neonatal CD301b + DC2 was specifically tied to heightened retinoic acid production, implicating this as a neonatally-enriched tolerance mechanism."
>
> — Antonin Weckel et al. (2022)

### T Helper Cell Polarization

DC2 support the differentiation of T helper cell subsets. There is evidence for their capacity to drive Th2 responses, and under some contexts, Th1 and Th17 responses, indicating versatility in immune modulation.

> "Other type of dendritic cells called DC2 promotes Th2 cells [13][14][15][63][64][65][66][67]."
>
> — B. Nedoszytko et al. (2017)

> "The T cell responses induced by DC2 are predominantly skewed toward a Th1/Th17 phenotype."
>
> — Bruna Clemente et al. (2023)

### Heterogeneity and Functional Diversity

Subsets of DC2 display functional heterogeneity, with certain populations expressing monocyte-associated markers and differing in antigen presentation and cytokine profiles.

> "When using CD1c to define DC2s, a CD14+ and a CD14− subset can be detected. The CD14+ subset shares features with monocytes, and this includes substantially higher expression levels for CD64, CD115, CD163, and S100A8/9."
>
> — L. Heger et al. (2020)

> "In conclusion, we observed additional heterogeneity among populations of dendritic cells, and observed that the identified subsets exhibited variable expression of several co-stimulatory molecules and chemokines."
>
> — Marte Heimli et al. (2023)

## Structure / Morphology

Evidence indicates that DC2 encompass subpopulations with varying expression of markers such as BDCA3 and monocyte-associated proteins. Subsets expressing BDCA3 show attributes of enhanced activation and cytokine production, while others display markers more typical of monocytes. Rat CD103- conventional DC2s, comparable to human DC2, are noted to be highly immunogenic.

> "A fraction of DC2 also express low to intermediate levels of BDCA3. ... mRNA fingerprints of BDCA3+ DC2 and BDCA3− DC2 are very similar, and clearly distinct from that of DC1."
>
> — E. Aa et al. (2018)

> "Newly found rat CD103- dendritic cells are the highly immunogenic conventional DC2 subpopulation, corresponding to the known DC subsets in mice and humans"
>
> — Y. Sawanobori et al. (2024)
