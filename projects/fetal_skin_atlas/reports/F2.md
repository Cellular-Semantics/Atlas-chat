# Fibroblast F2
Atlas: A prenatal skin atlas reveals immune regulation of human skin morphogenesis (DOI: 10.1038/s41586-024-08002-x)  
Scope: adult

## Summary

Fibroblast F2 is a distinct subtype within the fibroblast compartment of adult human skin, identified in high-resolution single-cell atlases by its unique molecular profile and anatomical distribution. Key markers include MFAP5, FBLN1, SFRP2, and CXCL14, which distinguish this population from other skin fibroblasts. Functionally, F2 fibroblasts are linked to extracellular matrix organization and immune signaling pathways, and are spatially enriched in submucosal regions of adult skin. Experimental and clinical studies associate F2-like fibroblasts with enhanced proliferation and specific morphologies, with potential implications in wound healing, tissue engineering, and tumor microenvironment dynamics.

## Markers

F2 fibroblasts are characterized by specific upregulation of several marker genes:

- **MFAP5 (Microfibril Associated Protein 5):** Identified through differential expression analysis, MFAP5 is implicated in microfibril assembly and extracellular matrix integrity, suggesting a structural role for F2 fibroblasts in dermal organization (Atlas Supplementary Table 3).
- **FBLN1 (Fibulin 1):** An ECM protein important for elastic fiber formation and tissue repair, highlighting a possible role in dermal elasticity and remodeling (Atlas Supplementary Table 3).
- **SFRP2 (Secreted Frizzled-Related Protein 2):** Modulates Wnt signaling involved in cell proliferation and tissue regeneration, supporting the regenerative potential of this fibroblast population (Atlas Supplementary Table 3).
- **CXCL14:** A chemokine associated with immune cell recruitment and regulation within tissue microenvironments, indicating a possible immunomodulatory role (Atlas Supplementary Table 3).

Supporting evidence notes the upregulation of these marker genes in F2 fibroblasts relative to other fibroblast populations in skin (Atlas Supplementary Table 3).

Additionally, other sources identify SFRP4, APOD, and PTGS2 as F2 markers in peri-epithelial fibroblasts of the prostate, which may share functional parallels:

> "F2 expressed genes associated with prostate peri-epithelial fibroblasts (APOD, PTGS2, SFRP4) (Joseph et al., 2021) (Figure 1I; Supplementary Figure S1F)."
>
> — Damisch et al. (2025)

Classic fibroblast markers (CD90, Fibronectin, CD44) are expressed similarly in F2 and F1, but F2 lacks CD34 expression, further defining its fibroblast identity:

> "no significant difference in fibroblast markers CD90, Fibronectin and CD44 between F1 and F2 cells, indicating that they are similar fibroblast cell populations for both viral and non-viral made iPSC-derived cells (Fig. 1B)."
>
> — Requena et al. (2019)

> "flow cytometry analysis of the F1 and F2 populations and confirmed that the two fibroblast cell populations are similar for two fibroblast cell markers CD90, CD44 and negative for stem cell marker CD34 (Fig. 1H)."
>
> — Requena et al. (2019)

## Location

### Skin (Adult)

F2 fibroblasts are predominantly found in the submucosa region of adult skin, highlighting a defined anatomical niche:

- F2 fibroblasts are enriched in the submucosa region in adult tissues (Atlas Supplementary Table 4).

Further evidence across tissue contexts suggests that F2-like fibroblasts are frequently located in peri-epithelial or sublining regions in other organs such as prostate and synovium, often in proximity to epithelial or immune structures:

> "cells expressing F2 markers APOD and SFRP4 were largely distinct from those expressing the F1 marker C7 in benignadjacent tissues (Supplementary Figure S2A) with APOD + /SFRP4 + cells typically in close proximity to epithelial glands whereas C7 + cells were frequently more distal, particularly within the interstitial stroma (Supplementary Figure S2A)."
>
> — Damisch et al. (2025)

> "The SC-F2 in particular was significantly increased in leukocyte-rich RA ssynovium compared to leukocyte-poor RA synovium and osteoarthritis (OA) synovium [10], suggesting these to encompass TLS-associated fibroblast subsets."
>
> — Lomholt et al. (2021)

## Function

### Extracellular Matrix Remodeling and Structural Support

The core markers of F2 fibroblasts (MFAP5, FBLN1, SFRP2) suggest a major role in extracellular matrix (ECM) assembly, elastin fiber formation, and matrix remodeling. This is aligned with spatial enrichment in the submucosa, a region critical for ECM organization (Atlas Supplementary Table 3, Table 4).

### Proliferation and Wound Healing

Experimental models demonstrate that F2-like fibroblasts show elevated proliferation rates and contribute to tissue repair:

> "The number of adhered fibroblasts on the F2 nanocoating was higher, and the cells tended to adopt a polygon-like shape (Figure 1, F2)."
>
> — Pajorova et al. (2018)

> "The enhancing effect of the fibrin mesh (F2) and of the Fn mesh (F2+Fn) on cell growth was evident mainly in the later cell cultivation intervals (Figure 1). On day 3 after cell seeding, several cell mitoses were observed on membranes with F2 and F2+Fn nanocoatings, but the cells remained mostly in their polygon-like shape (Figure 1)."
>
> — Pajorova et al. (2018)

> "The F2 nanocoating, characterized by a fine homogeneous nanofibrous mesh, results in fibroblasts adopting a polygonal shape and exhibiting a higher proliferation rate compared to F1 nanocoating."
>
> — Bačáková et al. (2019)

> "The cytoproliferative dose-responses on the human dermal fibroblasts were in the ranking order of F5~F6 > F2 > F4~F3 > F1, which was in general agreement with the residual bioactive FGF-2 content... The maximal cytoproliferative activity of F2, F3, F4, F5 and F6 were higher than F1, which might suggest that the effects of FGF-2 on the human dermal fibroblasts was potentiated by the stabilizers."
>
> — Benington et al. (2024)

### Immunomodulation

F2 fibroblasts display upregulation of immune-related pathways and interact with immune cells, as evidenced by chemokine (CXCL14) and immune system protein expression:

- The main differences in protein expression in F2 cells are signal transduction, cell cycle, and immune system proteins (Requena et al., 2019).
- F2 upregulates immune-related proteins and participates in local signaling environments.

### Tumor Microenvironment and Fibroblast Polarization

The F2 fibroblast identity extends into cancer biology, where the "F2" state refers to a tumor-promoting cancer-associated fibroblast (CAF) polarization:

> "CAF... F2 refers to type II-CAFs that exhibit tumor-promoting properties."
>
> — Augsten (2014)

> "F1 and F2 represent the two extremes of a spectrum of different phenotypes that CAFs can adopt and (2) the polarization of CAFs stays under the control of the tumor (micro)environment."
>
> — Augsten (2014)

### Signal Transduction

F2 fibroblasts demonstrate heightened signal transduction activity, particularly upregulation of AKT pathways, which may underpin their proliferative and survival advantages:

> "signal transduction pathways for AKT is over expressed in F2 cells compared to F1 cells by Western blot analysis (Fig. 1I)."
>
> — Requena et al. (2019)

## Structure / Morphology

F2 fibroblasts adopt a distinct polygonal cell shape, especially in engineered or in vitro ECM-mimetic environments.

> "While the cells on the F1 nanocoating remained in a spindle-like shape, a polygon-like shape was typical for the cells cultivated on the F2 nanocoating."
>
> — Pajorova et al. (2018)

> "The fibroblasts on the F1 nanocoating remained in their typical spindle-like shape, while the cells on the F2 nanocoating were polygonal with a higher proliferation rate [122]."
>
> — Bačáková et al. (2019)

The F2 nanocoating itself forms a fine fibrin mesh more closely mimicking natural ECM:

> "The fine nanofibrous fibrin mesh of the F2 nanocoating resembled a 2-D surface, but its nanostructure mimicked the natural ECM, enabling faster and more successful adhesion of cells through adhesion receptors."
>
> — Pajorova et al. (2018)

This mesh is remodeled by fibroblasts over time, demonstrating their active participation in ECM turnover:

> "If we compare F1 and F2 nanocoatings, it seems that the fine nanofibrous fibrin mesh of the F2 nanocoating was formed into a more physiologic structure, which enabled the fibroblasts to remodel it and replace it with their own ECM."
>
> — Pajorova et al. (2018)
